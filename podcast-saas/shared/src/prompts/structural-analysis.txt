━━━ STEP 1: THINK DEEPLY BEFORE YOU STRUCTURE ━━━

Before touching the JSON, answer these questions internally:

• What everyday moment or object does the listener already know that secretly involves this topic? (A stadium wave, a fridge magnet, a traffic jam — something they've seen or felt.)
• What is the single most surprising or counterintuitive fact that most people don't know?
• What makes this subject actually matter to a human being — not academically, but emotionally or practically?
• What's the hidden human story inside the technical/abstract topic?
• What analogy from everyday life makes the hardest concept click instantly?
• What genuine tension or unresolved debate lives inside this topic?
• What are the 6–10 most concrete intellectual anchors — names, dates, experiments, historical moments, methods, limitations, applications, surprising facts?
• What single metaphor can carry the whole episode, deepening at each beat?
• What question or contradiction at the end of each beat naturally creates the next one?

Use all those answers to build the structure.

━━━ STEP 2: PRODUCE THE STRUCTURE JSON ━━━

The narrative_arc must follow this 6-beat structure (adapt proportions to target duration):
1. RELEVANCE — opens in the listener's world (hook_scenario is the opening)
2. SURPRISE — the counterintuitive reveal that makes them lean in
3. FOUNDATION — minimum necessary background, no more than needed
4. COMPLEXITY — tensions, edge cases, "but wait..." moments
5. APPLICATIONS — why this matters right now, in the real world
6. PHILOSOPHY — an open question the listener carries out the door

For short (~5 min): compress to 4 beats (merge Foundation+Complexity, drop Applications or Philosophy).
For long (~25 min): expand Complexity with 2–3 sub-beats.

Each beat must end with a curiosity handoff — a question or contradiction that pulls the next beat forward naturally.

Output a JSON object matching this schema exactly:

CRITICAL RULES:
- hook_scenario: NEVER start with the topic name or "In this episode..." — always start in the listener's world
- topic_map: 3–6 entries — the right depth for the target duration, not padded
- knowledge_anchors: 6–10 entries for medium/long episodes; every anchor must be concrete enough to speak aloud naturally; reject vague anchors like "this is important"
- metaphor_spine: choose a primary metaphor that can work at the opening, deepen mid-episode, and transform at the ending; do not choose a metaphor that only fits one moment
- narrative_arc and pacing_seconds must be the same length
- pacing_seconds values are ROUGH ESTIMATES; exact timing comes from TTS rendering
- pacing_seconds should sum to approximately {{TARGET_MIN}} × 60
- Every topic_map entry needs ≥1 genuine tension and ≥1 vivid, specific analogy
- Analogies must be concrete ("like a casino where the house always wins your attention" not "like a complex system")
- Tensions must be real disagreements or surprises, not obvious observations
- Title must be specific to THIS topic — not a generic podcast title template
- Do not invent facts not present in the source material or well-established knowledge
- CRITICAL: Output ONLY the raw JSON object — no preamble, no commentary, no markdown. Start with { and end with }
